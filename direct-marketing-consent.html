Terminology

What do we mean when we say certain words.

Direct marketing

Refers to all communication being sent by the CRM team via email to the customer. This can be news, offers or recommendations.

Personalised ads

Refers to sharing user registration data for marketing purposes. E.g Storytel ads more apparent on social media channels.

Soft opt-in

Refers to a more lenient regulations towards consent in certain market. For instance possibility to pre-checking the consentbox for the user.

Strict opt-in

For some markets we need to be more aware of the legality of direct marketing. In these markets we should leave the consent boxes unchecked and let the user actively opt in by themself.

Assumed consent

In some very lenient markets we have the option to not even ask the user for their consent, and just assume their consent on signup.

Problem statement

What are we trying to solve?

The current implementation

At the time the direct marketing consent was merged together with the create account buttons as well as the terms & conditions on the create account screen.

- It competed with the primary CTA's

- It was not prominent enough.

Discovery

When we jumped into discovery there was one question that was present at all times. - "How do we improve the consent rates without of negatively impacting the user experience?" It was important for us that we did not fall into dark patterns in the UX. We want more user to opt in but we want users who are truly finding a value of getting news and book recommendations in the email inbox.

Throughout the discovery we started formulating 3 hypothesis that would lead to become the solution we pursued to validate.

Make information prominent

We don't serve us or the user when displaying the information in small print, complicated legal copy or by trying to trick the user to consent.

Removing cognitive load

Signing up requires many actions from a user and we risk missing the users that are interested in direct marketing when we let that action compete with SSO buttons and primary CTA's

Adding delight

By adding delight in terms of micro animations or illustrated elements we turn an otherwise mundane decision into a more interesting experience, we make the user stop a minute to really think if this is something interested for them.

The Solution

We launched a project to completely overhaul the consent experience. Our approach was twofold:

Differentiated UX

We tailored the UX to comply with the specific legislation of each market. This meant implementing different flows for "opt-in," "opt-out," and "assumed consent" markets.

Optimized UX

We ran an experiment to test new user interface (UI) designs. The winning solution was to move the direct marketing consent checkbox to a separate screen that appeared after the account creation step. This change significantly reduced clutter and cognitive load, putting a clear focus on the consent decision.

The Results

The project's success was immediate and significant. We not only improved our opt-in rate but also saw no negative impact on our core subscription conversion rates.
