Improving opt-in rates

For Direct Marketing

A/B Testing Conversion Optimization UX/UI

Terminology

What do we mean when we say certain words.

Direct marketing

Refers to all communication being sent by the CRM team via email to the customer. This can be news, offers or recommendations.

Personalised ads

Refers to sharing user registration data for marketing purposes. E.g Storytel ads more apparent on social media channels.

Problem statement

At the time the direct marketing consent was merged together with the create account buttons as well as the terms & conditions on the create account screen.

- It competed with the primary CTA's

- It was not prominent enough.

Discovery

When we jumped into discovery, one question remained present throughout: 'How do we improve consent rates without negatively impacting the user experience? It was critical that we avoided dark patterns in the UX. While we wanted more users to opt in, we only wanted users who genuinely valued receiving news and book recommendations in their inbox.

Throughout discovery, we formulated 3 hypotheses that would become the solutions we pursued and validated.

Make the information prominent

Small print, complicated legal jargon, and deceptive tactics serve neither us nor our users.

Removing cognitive load

When sign-up competes with SSO buttons and primary CTAs, we risk losing users who genuinely want to receive our communications.

Adding user delight

Adding delight through micro-animations and illustrations transforms an otherwise mundane decision into something worth pausing for—giving users a moment to genuinely reflect on their interest.

The Solution

An improved sign-up flow with dedicated space for users to consider both account creation and opt-in preferences.

The Results

The project's success was immediate and significant. We not only improved our opt-in rate but also saw no negative impact on our core subscription conversion rates.
